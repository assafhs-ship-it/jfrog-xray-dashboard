CS Playbook — Intervention by Stage
Recommended actions to move customers to the next stage
Stage 0 — Dormant
Trigger the First Value Moment
  • Run a scan on top 5 repos; send a "here's what we found" report
  • Send a post-CVE news alert (Log4Shell, XZ Utils) referencing their stack
  • Schedule a 45-min "Xray Activation Sprint" with a pre-built policy template
  • Identify an internal security champion to drive adoption from within
Stage 1 — Configured / Ignored
Turn Noise into Signal
  • Review configured policies — are thresholds too low causing alert fatigue?
  • Enable CVE prioritization: focus on Critical + exploitable only
  • Map top 3 CVEs to remediation steps and assign to a dev backlog
  • Set up a weekly digest instead of per-alert emails
Stage 2 — Active / Shallow
Enforce at the Gate
  • Connect Xray to CI/CD pipeline as a blocking quality gate
  • Expand scan coverage: add remaining repos and container images
  • Introduce license compliance policies for legal risk reduction
  • Show security posture benchmarks vs. similar customers (anonymized)
Stage 3 — Embedded
Expand & Advocate
  • Introduce Advanced Security: secrets detection, SAST, contextual analysis
  • Build a security KPI report for CISO / board visibility
  • Identify as a reference customer / case study candidate
  • Upsell to JFrog Advanced Security or Curation
Customer Adoption Details
Click column headers to sort
Customer ↕
CustomerCompany name and industry. The primary identifier for this account in your CS portfolio.
Tier ↕
TierCustomer segment — Fortune 100, Enterprise, or SMB. Used to prioritize CS effort and tailor the right adoption playbook to account size and complexity.
Stage ↕
Adoption StageWhere the customer is in their Xray journey:
  • 0 — Dormant: installed, nothing configured
  • 1 — Configured: policies set, alerts ignored
  • 2 — Active: scanning, not blocking pipelines
  • 3 — Embedded: fully pipeline-integrated
Health Score ↕
Health Score (0–100)A composite adoption score calculated from four signals:
  • Repos scanned / total (30 pts)
  • Policies configured (30 pts)
  • Scanned in last 7 days (20 pts)
  • CVEs being actioned (20 pts)
Lower score = more urgent CS intervention needed.
Repos Scanned ↕
Repos ScannedNumber of Artifactory repositories actively scanned by Xray out of the customer's total. Low coverage = blind spots in their security posture. 100% is the target.
Policies ↕
Policies ConfiguredThe number of active security policies set up in Xray. Zero policies means no alerts are ever triggered — this is the single most critical gap for Stage 0 customers. Even one meaningful policy counts as a first activation milestone.
Open CVEs ↕
Open CVEsUnresolved vulnerabilities found in scanned artifacts:
  • CRIT: actively exploitable — fix immediately
  • HIGH: serious risk, prioritize next sprint
A high count with a low health score signals the customer is scanning but not acting — a CS intervention opportunity.
Last Scan ↕
Last ScanDays since the last Xray scan ran on this account. A stale scan means the customer's security view is outdated:
  • 0–6 days: healthy
  • 7–29 days: attention needed
  • 30+ days: urgent — flag for CS outreach
Renewal ↕
RenewalDays until this customer's license renewal. Customers at Stage 0 or 1 with fewer than 90 days to renewal are at highest churn risk — they have not seen value and have little reason to renew. Immediate CS action required.
CS Owner ↕
CS OwnerThe Customer Success Manager responsible for this account's adoption journey. Use the filter bar above to view only your own portfolio.
Action
Recommended ActionA contextual next step for the CS rep, based on the customer's current adoption stage:
  • Activate (Stage 0): run first scan, schedule activation sprint
  • Unblock (Stage 1): fix policy config, reduce CVE noise
  • Embed (Stage 2): connect CI/CD gate, expand coverage
  • Expand (Stage 3): upsell Advanced Security, build CISO report
